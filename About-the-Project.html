About me

I am an architect by training, with a specialisation in conservation of built heritage. I have had over a decade of experience working in India, focusing on urban regeneration, disasters and heritage. This website is a small archive that supports my doctoral research at Institute for Culture and Society, Western Sydney University. I examine post-disaster reconstruction practices for heritage in Bhaktapur, Nepal following the 2015 Gorkha Earthquake. I can be contacted at vanickaarora@gmail.com Follow me at https://twitter.com/VanickaA

My doctoral work has been generously supported by a Research and Training Programme Scholarship (2018-2021) offered by the Australian Government. My fieldwork has been supported by Institute for Culture and Society’s candidature support funding.

The design and layout of this website and its graphics has been supported by funds from Landscape Research Group.

I would like to acknowledge the invaluable contributions of Dr. Hayley Saul, Prof. Emma Waterton, and Prof. Denis Byrne at Western Sydney University who have supervised my doctoral work and offered me unflinching support through what has been an exceptionally strange set of circumstances!

She loves traditional culture, arts, and architecture and is always looking forward to work in the fields of art. She is skilled at translation and background research specifically in relation to cultural studies.
